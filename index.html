Experience

IIT Research Institute, Research Support Services, Report Writing, Life Sciences Group: Associate Technical Writer, since June 2019; Wilbur Wright College, City Colleges of Chicago, Adjunct Professor of English, August 2018- May 2019; Hotel Amerika Literary Journal, Assistant Editor, since January 2015; Columbia College, Graduate Student Instructor, January 2015- May 2017; Smithers Viscient LLC, Field Scientist I, December 2014- November 2016; BFF Bikes LLC, Mechanic, September 2014-May 2019; University of Iowa College of Public Health, Research Assistant, September 2009- October 2011; Bikeblogordie.com, Founder and developer, since October 2008

Mr. Wilson currently serves as an Associate Technical Writer. His responsibilities include generating standard operating procedures as well as reviewing and re-issuing historic procedures and protocols, under the direction of division managers. In addition, he distributes reading material to employees through an internal database and files documents in physical and digital form. Software programs used in the course of these duties include several component programs of Microsoft office and Adobe Acrobat X Pro and DC, and additional database software.

Previously, Mr. Wilson served as an Adjunct Professor of English with the City Colleges of Chicago where he designed a syllabus, complete with reading and writing assignments from dozens of source materials, and conducted 101 and 102 classes, in accordance with City College values and regulations. Prior to becoming a professor he completed a graduate instructor theory and praxis course through Columbia College and taught five semesters of college English there, following a syllabus and assignment scheme designed and tested by Mr. Wilson and reviewed by the Dean of Instruction.

At the Pushcart Award-winning literary journal, Hotel Amerika, Mr. Wilson screened stories, essays, and poems for publication eligibility. He also proofread accepted pieces and line-edited completed publishing galleys. Hotel Amerika publishes out of the University of Nebraska Press and is distributed internationally in print.

During his time with Smithers Viscient, a toxicology research firm out of North Carolina, Mr. Wilson traveled across Florida, Wisconsin, and California to collect biological samples for laboratory testing, in service of regulatory studies that involved threatened commercial honeybee operations and chemical exposure. He worked independently and in groups while coordinating with chemical suppliers and farm owners to obtain samples in a manner consistent with study protocols, in all weather conditions, while maintaining personal safety and anti-contamination standards.

Finally, while working for the University of Iowa’s College of Public Health, Mr. Wilson lead a group of research associates to distribute marketing material and conduct surveys regarding reproductive health across the state of Iowa. Responsibilities included reserving vehicles and accommodation, coordinating with event management at distribution sites, and preparing results for analyzation through coding software
